How Third-Party Risk Assessment works

This is a retrieval-grounded (RAG) assistant, not a black-box model. It reads only the vendor's own documents and evaluates each control against them — a human analyst reviews and signs off every determination.

📚 Per-vendor evidence indexing

Each assessment gets its own slice of the evidence index. Every uploaded document — SOC 2 reports, policies, the Aravo export — is broken into passages and indexed by meaning. There is no document-count limit; the chunking and embedding happen on our infrastructure.

🔎 Evidence-only control evaluation

For each baseline control, the most relevant passages from that vendor's evidence are retrieved and re-ranked, then handed to the model as the only source it may use. The standing instruction:

Use ONLY the vendor evidence provided. Do NOT infer or speculate. The absence of evidence is valid assessment data — if evidence does not support the control, the determination is "Not Met" (lack of evidence, NOT a control failure). Use "Not Applicable" only when clearly out of scope. Never invent dates, names, versions, or counts.

If retrieval comes back below the relevance threshold, the control is marked Not Met with the standardized "not provided" statement — no answer is invented from thin evidence.

⚖️ Risk synthesis

Once controls are evaluated, the assistant proposes inherent and residual risk ratings plus a neutral, executive-ready overall statement, derived only from the determinations. The analyst can edit all three.

👤 Human in control

The assistant only ever produces a draft. Every determination, every rating, and the final sign-off stay with the analyst. The exported DD Form fills every field — including explicit "not provided" statements — so nothing is left blank.
