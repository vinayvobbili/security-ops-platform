How Does the GDnR Wiki Work?

📖

What is this?

The GDnR Wiki is a knowledge garden built from your team's incident response runbooks and procedures. It takes the original Word documents from the corporate wiki and transforms them into a searchable, cross-linked, tagged knowledge base.

🤖

How are articles created?

Source documents (DOCX, PDF, etc.) are uploaded to the Docs Library, then compiled by a local LLM into structured markdown articles. The compiler extracts key facts, builds tables, and adds cross-links to related articles. Click Compile to process new documents.

🏷️

Tags & Filtering

Every article is auto-tagged by category (phishing, malware, credential theft), platform (CrowdStrike, Tanium, QRadar), threat actor (Scattered Spider, APT41), and tool (Impacket, TruffleHog). Use the tag chips on the home page to filter, or click a tag on any article to find related runbooks.

🔗

Cross-Links & Backlinks

Articles reference each other with [[wikilinks]], rendered as clickable blue chips in the text. At the bottom of each article, the Backlinks panel shows every other article that links to the current one — so you can always trace connections in both directions.

🕸️

Knowledge Graph

Click the Graph button to open a full-screen interactive visualization of the entire wiki. Each node is an article, colored by its primary tag group. Edges represent wikilinks between articles. Bigger nodes have more connections.

  • Hover a node to see its full title and highlight its connections
  • Click a node to navigate to that article
  • Drag nodes to rearrange the layout

Search

The search bar matches against article titles, tags, and body content. Title matches are boosted so the most relevant runbook surfaces first. Results show tag chips and a snippet for quick scanning.
