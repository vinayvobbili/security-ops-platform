💡 How Active Threat Intake works

Three desks, three questions. This is the adversary desk.

🎯 What it answers

"An actor or campaign is active — what are their indicators, and are they already in our environment?" Cyber Security Advisories handles the asset axis (a vulnerable package we own); this handles the threat axis (an active adversary), regardless of whether a CVE is involved.

🔬 How a report becomes a queue entry

  • You paste any threat report.
  • A local model extracts the actor, campaign, threat type, severity, a tight summary, the in-the-wild IOCs, the MITRE ATT&CK techniques, and recommended actions.
  • A non-semantic backstop sweeps the raw text for any IOCs the model missed (defanged URLs, hashes, IPs) so nothing slips through.
  • The structured threat lands in the queue, deduped by content so re-pasting the same report doesn't stack.

🛣 What's coming next

  • Enrichment — IOC reputation from VirusTotal / AbuseIPDB / urlscan / Recorded Future.
  • Hunt wire — auto-queue a threat hunt to check whether those indicators touched our telemetry.
  • Block wire — one-click block of confirmed-malicious indicators.
  • Auto-ingest — pull active-actor intel automatically so the queue fills itself.
