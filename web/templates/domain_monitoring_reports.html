How this report works

Every day, automated scans discover domains that may impersonate a the company brand — three ways:

  • Lookalike generation — permutations of each brand's primary domain (typo-squats, swaps, homoglyphs).
  • Recorded Future watchlist — the brand-keyword domains RF tracks, enriched with risk scores.
  • Certificate Transparency sweep — new TLS certificates containing a brand keyword, across every spelling variant.

Each discovered domain lands in a findings ledger. Analysts then triage it — confirm the brand, mark it relevant or irrelevant, and (for malicious sites) raise a takedown. Raising a takedown opens a PhishFort incident and records its ID here automatically.

This monthly report rolls the ledger up: impersonations by brand, the takedown-versus-monitoring split, the weekly discovery trend, and the full triaged list — downloadable as Excel or PDF.

Coordinated Campaigns group findings that share an actor's footprint — the same hosting IP, WHOIS registrant, or authoritative nameserver. Bulk registrars, Let's Encrypt, and shared CDNs are ignored (they connect unrelated domains), so a cluster reflects genuinely linked registrations — usually one actor — that can be investigated and taken down as a batch rather than one at a time.
