How Does Meeting Recap Work?

Quick walkthrough — upload to recap in under 2 minutes.
📤

1. Upload

Upload an audio or video file of your meeting. Supported formats include MP3, WAV, M4A, MP4, MKV, MOV, and more. Select the meeting type — Incident Bridge or Team Meeting — to get a summary tailored to the context.

🎬

2. Audio Extraction

If you uploaded a video container (MP4, MKV, etc.), ffmpeg automatically extracts the audio track and converts it to 16kHz mono WAV — the optimal format for transcription accuracy.

🎧

3. Speech-to-Text

OpenAI's Whisper large-v3-turbo (a 809M-parameter speech recognition model) transcribes the audio. It auto-detects the language and handles accents, background noise, and technical jargon. Runs locally on Apple Silicon — nothing leaves your infrastructure.

🗣️

4. Speaker Diarization

Pyannote 3.1 identifies individual speakers and assigns labels (SPEAKER_00, SPEAKER_01, etc.). Each transcript segment is tagged with the speaker who has the most overlap with that time window. You can rename speakers to real names after processing.

🧠

5. AI Summary

The diarized transcript is sent to an LLM which produces a structured JSON summary. The output varies by meeting type:

  • Incident Bridge — executive summary, timeline of events, decisions, action items with owners, and open questions
  • Team Meeting — overview, topics with key points, decisions, and action items with owners
  • Customer Requirements — Solutions Architect's view: requirements (with must/should/nice priority), pain points, success criteria, stakeholders, action items, and open questions
✏️

6. Review & Edit

Click any SPEAKER_NN label in the transcript to rename it (e.g., "Alice"). All instances update at once and the mapping is saved. Use "Compare with my notes" to cross-check the transcript against your own meeting minutes in the Meeting Q&A tool.

🌐

7. Multilingual Summaries

Pick a language at upload time to get the summary in that language straight from the first pass, or translate an existing English summary in place from the results panel. Supported languages: English, Spanish, French, German, Italian, Portuguese, Dutch, Russian, Chinese (Simplified), Japanese, Korean, Hindi, Arabic (13 total). Speaker labels (SPEAKER_00), ISO dates, and priority tokens (must-have / should-have / nice-to-have) stay in their original form so downstream tooling keeps working.

🎞️

8. Video Recap (optional)

Flip the Generate Video Recap? toggle on the upload form to also get a narrated MP4 of the summary — perfect for skimming a one-hour meeting in about ninety seconds. Once it's ready, it appears as an embedded player at the top of the recap with a download button. The pipeline:

  • Slides — PIL renders one slide per section (cover, summary, topics / timeline / requirements, decisions, action items, open questions, outro), styled to the corporate cybersecurity slide template — navy covers with a blue ribbon, the company wordmark, page counter, and a section badge ("03 | TOPICS") on every slide.
  • Bullets vs. narration — slide bullets stay short (just the headline). The narration is the prose version that weaves in owners, due dates, and rationales ("Marcus will own the rollout by April 25 — rationale: ...").
  • Voice — per-slide TTS via Kokoro on the inference Mac (en, es, fr, it, pt, ja, zh, hi), with gTTS as an automatic fallback for the other locales (de, nl, ru, ko, ar) or if the Kokoro tunnel is down. Section labels are localized in all 13 supported languages.
  • Compose — ffmpeg muxes the slides + per-slide audio with a brand-blue gradient and bed music, fading out over the outro card.

The Prompts

These are the exact instructions given to the LLM to generate each summary type.

🎯

System Instruction

You are a SOC incident analyst summarizing a recorded incident bridge call. Output ONLY valid JSON — no markdown, no preamble, no explanation. Be concise and fact-only. Distinguish decisions made, actions assigned, and the timeline of events. Use the speaker labels from the transcript verbatim when assigning action items.

📋

Required Output Fields

  • title — short descriptive title (max 80 chars)
  • summary — 3–5 sentence executive summary of what happened and current status
  • timeline — array of {time, speaker, event} capturing the sequence of events
  • decisions — array of {decision, decided_by, rationale}
  • action_items — array of {action, owner, due} with speaker labels as owners
  • open_questions — unresolved items raised but not answered
🎯

System Instruction

You are an executive assistant summarizing a recorded team meeting. Output ONLY valid JSON — no markdown, no preamble, no explanation. Capture agenda topics discussed, decisions reached, and action items with clear owners. Use the speaker labels from the transcript verbatim when assigning action items.

📋

Required Output Fields

  • title — short descriptive title (max 80 chars)
  • summary — 3–5 sentence overview of what the team discussed
  • topics — array of {topic, key_points[]} capturing agenda items and themes
  • decisions — array of {decision, rationale}
  • action_items — array of {action, owner, due} with speaker labels as owners
🎯

System Instruction

You are a Senior Solutions Architect listening to a recorded customer discovery or requirements call. Your job is to extract the customer's stated requirements, pain points, and success criteria from the conversation so an engineering team can act on them. Output ONLY valid JSON — no markdown, no preamble, no explanation. Be precise and evidence-based — only capture what was explicitly stated or clearly implied; do not invent features the customer did not ask for. Use the speaker labels from the transcript verbatim when attributing requirements, concerns, or action items.

📋

Required Output Fields

  • title — short descriptive title (max 80 chars)
  • summary — 3–5 sentence executive summary of the customer's situation and ask
  • requirements — array of {requirement, priority, raised_by, rationale} (priority: must-have / should-have / nice-to-have)
  • pain_points — array of {pain, impact}
  • success_criteria — how the customer will judge the solution a success
  • stakeholders — array of {speaker, role, concerns}
  • action_items — array of {action, owner, due}
  • open_questions — items to follow up on
⚙️

Infrastructure

Transcription model: Whisper large-v3-turbo (faster-whisper, int8 quantized)
Diarization model: pyannote/speaker-diarization-3.1
Summary model: Local LLM on Apple Silicon
Video pipeline: PIL slide renderer + Kokoro TTS (with gTTS fallback) + ffmpeg compose
Privacy: All processing runs on internal infrastructure — no data leaves the network
Storage: Transcripts, summaries & rendered videos kept on disk; audio files auto-deleted after 30 days
Performance: ~5–15 min for a 30-min recording (varies with audio quality and speaker count); video adds another ~1–2 min
