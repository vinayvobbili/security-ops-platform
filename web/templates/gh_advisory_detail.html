How Does an Advisory Investigation Work?

🙋

1. Take ownership

Click Assign to me so it's clear who's driving. Unowned advisories auto-archive after 3 days — assigning keeps it in the live queue. The discussion thread at the bottom captures who said what, and when.

🧭

2. Read the CAPD scorecard

A grounded “do we declare a CAPD?” verdict scored from data we already pull — EPSS, CISA KEV, CVSS, our software exposure (Veracode/JFrog), active-threat intel (Recorded Future), internet reachability (Shodan), and patch availability. It replaces worst-case guessing with a defensible 0–100 score and band.

🧰

3. Run the capabilities you need

Owner-clickable checks against the SOC's tooling: JFrog/Veracode (do we ship the affected component?), Threat Intel (Recorded Future), CrowdStrike + QRadar (“were we touched?”), Hunt Workbench, and AttackIQ. Results persist on the advisory and flow into the report.

❓

4. Check the FAQ & ask the assistant

The CAPD decision FAQ answers the questions that drive the call (declare? easy to exploit? are we affected?). The chat bubble can answer anything about this advisory — exposure, packages, whether to escalate.

🚦

5. Decide & record

Capture your call in the triage notes, then either Close — not worth reporting or Notify the Package Compromise Assessment Teams channel (duplicate-safe). Download a report for a self-contained record of everything you found.

📣 Notify {{ teams_channel }} Team

Review and edit the draft below, then click Notify to post it to the {{ teams_channel }} Teams channel. Sending also marks this advisory as reported (duplicate-safe).

🎫 Create XSOAR ticket

Review and edit the incident description below, then click Create ticket to raise an XSOAR incident. One ticket per advisory (duplicate-safe).
