🔭 How the Threat Hunt Workbench works

This is the same engine that runs automatically on every piece of threat intel we ingest — exposed here as a hunter-in-the-loop tool so you can point it at any report, on demand.

1️⃣ Extract

The text is parsed for indicators (IPs, domains, URLs, file names, hashes, CVEs), malware families, threat actors and MITRE ATT&CK technique IDs.

2️⃣ “Were we touched?” — live IOC fan-out

Every indicator is searched across the security tools you select (QRadar, CrowdStrike, XSIAM, Abnormal) over your chosen lookback window. Hits come back with the hosts and sources that matched.

3️⃣ Behavioral TTP hunts

Beyond known indicators, the in-house LLM authors behavioral hunt queries (CrowdStrike LogScale & XSIAM XQL) from the narrative, validates them, and executes them live — catching attacker behavior even when the specific IOCs differ.

4️⃣ “Can we detect this?” — coverage map

The report's ATT&CK techniques are checked against our live detection-rule catalog (QRadar / CrowdStrike / Tanium) to show what's covered vs. the gaps worth closing.

5️⃣ Verdict

A short verdict and prioritized next actions tie it together. Everything is read-only — no blocking, quarantine, or detonation — and every run is logged with an audit trail and saved to the shared hunt history.
